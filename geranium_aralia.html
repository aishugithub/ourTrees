The Ornamental Air Purifier with Healing Energy

Basic Information

Is the Geranium Aralia native to India?
No, Geranium Aralia (Polyscias fruticosa), also known as Ming Aralia, is not native to India. It is native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands, but is widely grown as an ornamental and air-purifying plant in India and around the world.

Geranium Aralia, also known as Ming Aralia, is a graceful ornamental plant with finely cut foliage that resembles tiny parsley leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it is widely appreciated as an air-purifying indoor plant. Its presence is believed to bring positive energy and serenity to homes and workspaces.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Polyscias
  • Species: P. fruticosa

Common Names

  • Geranium Aralia
  • Ming Aralia
  • Chinese Aralia

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 1-2 meters indoors
  • Leaves: Lacy, dark green, finely divided
  • Growth Habit: Upright, bushy
  • Type: Tropical evergreen shrub

Uses & Benefits

Air Purification

Effectively removes indoor pollutants and purifies the air. Ideal for homes and offices to maintain fresh and healthy indoor environments.

Traditional Use

Used in folk medicine in Southeast Asia for treating infections and promoting digestive health.

Feng Shui

Believed to attract prosperity and calmness. Often placed in entryways and corners for positive energy flow.

Cultivation & Care

Light

Thrives in bright, indirect light. Can tolerate partial shade but avoid harsh direct sunlight.

Watering

Keep soil slightly moist. Water when the top inch feels dry. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Soil

Prefers well-draining potting mix. Add coco peat or perlite for better aeration.

Pruning

Prune occasionally to maintain shape and encourage bushy growth. Remove any yellowing leaves.
