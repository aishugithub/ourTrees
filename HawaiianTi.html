Hawaiian Ti Plant

Cordyline fruticosa

The Plant of Protection and Prosperity

Basic Information

The Hawaiian Ti Plant (Cordyline fruticosa) is a tropical evergreen known for its vibrant foliage in shades of green, pink, red, or purple. Native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands, this plant is deeply woven into Polynesian culture and is believed to bring good luck, protection, and positive energy. In Hawaii, it's a common sight in gardens and rituals.

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Asparagaceae
  • Genus: Cordyline
  • Species: C. fruticosa

Common Names

  • Good Luck Plant
  • Ti Plant
  • Cabbage Palm
  • Red Sister (cultivar)

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 3 to 10 ft
  • Leaves: Lance-shaped, glossy, colorful
  • Flowers: Small, pink to purple
  • Type: Evergreen shrub

Uses & Cultural Significance

Traditional Uses

  • Used in lei and ceremonial garlands
  • Leaves wrap food in cooking (like laulau)
  • Used in spiritual cleansing rituals
  • Symbol of protection and luck

Medicinal Uses

  • Root extracts used traditionally for fever
  • Leaves applied to soothe skin irritation
  • Used in poultices and tonics

Feng Shui & Beliefs

  • Attracts prosperity and wealth
  • Planted at entrances for protection
  • Red Ti often preferred for energy flow

Care & Cultivation

Sunlight

Prefers bright, indirect sunlight. Can tolerate partial shade. Too much direct sunlight may scorch leaves.

Watering

Keep soil consistently moist, not soggy. Avoid letting the plant dry out completely. Mist leaves in dry weather.

Soil

Well-draining, fertile soil with organic matter. Slightly acidic to neutral pH preferred.

Propagation

Easy to propagate via stem cuttings. Cut a stem section, let it dry slightly, and plant in moist soil.

Interesting Facts

Hula Tradition

Ti leaves are essential in traditional Hawaiian hula costumes and performances.

Edible Roots

Roots were once baked and used as a source of sugar or alcohol by early Polynesians.

Symbol of Peace

In Hawaiian legend, planting a Ti plant marks the beginning of a new life or new peace.
