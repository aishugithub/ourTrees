Firecracker Flower

Crossandra infundibuliformis

The Fiery Bloom of South India

Basic Information

Firecracker Flower, or *Crossandra infundibuliformis*, is a vibrant, evergreen shrub native to southern India and Sri Lanka. With its bright orange, coral, or salmon-colored blooms, it is a favorite in tropical gardens and traditional Indian rituals. Its name is inspired by the crackling sound its dried seed pods make when they burst open.

Cultivation & Care

Sunlight

Prefers full sun to partial shade. Needs warmth and bright light to bloom prolifically.

Watering

Requires regular watering. Keep soil moist but not soggy. Do not let it dry out completely.

Soil

Grows well in well-drained, fertile loamy soil. Enrich with compost for better results.

Pruning

Prune faded blooms and leggy stems to encourage bushy growth and more flowering.

Uses & Significance

Religious Uses

Commonly used in South Indian temples for garlands, offerings, and ceremonies.

Ornamental Use

Popular as a border plant or in decorative pots for homes, balconies, and verandahs.

Cosmetic Use

Adored by women for hair decoration, especially during festivals and traditional functions.
